On Home

What is Home?

Home, for me, is the other side. In German it's called Jenseits — literally, "beyond." Coming back home means that most of the veil is lifted. We remember that everything is okay. We remember the bigger picture. We remember that we are loved unconditionally. We remember we can have a daily connection with our immediate guides. We remember that we are a soul within a soul family.

It means we can live in two layers of consciousness at the same time — the one that experiences the specific physical and emotional life here, and the one that remembers we designed this stage to learn something, and to come back home. A full circle to inner peace. Wiser. More appreciative of life. More in love with our unique qualities — qualities we couldn't have appreciated without going on the journey first.

The challenges of life are like going away on a long trip. But if you never left your bed — if you never left your doorstep — how would you ever get to experience the bliss of coming back home? The shower after the journey. The fresh sheets. The feeling of: I made it. How would you get to know yourself and your abilities if you never challenged anything?

Living with a consciousness of home means being able to see someone — especially someone you love, even when you're mad at them — and seeing the soul that they are.

Their fears. Their passions and wishes and strengths. The mirror they are to you. And being able to feel grateful for the growth they represent. Home is being able to feel layers of love that, without being connected to your higher self, to the divine origin of your heart, you couldn't even imagine existed.

Home is seeing that we are in this together — more than we could ever imagine. Home is feeling calm at the eye of the storm, because something deep inside you tells you: you are at the right place, at the right time. Home is already having the experience that when you listened to your inner voice — to your intuition — even when others told you that you were crazy, it paid off.

Home is being able to gaze at a mountain and feel love, and feel it love you back. Home is feeling the love from the heart of the earth. Home is being able to appreciate this moment. Like in the song Home by Nicholas Bamberger: home is here, home is now.

And one more thing.

Home does not mean the journey is over. Home doesn't mean life's challenges are done and everything is effortless. In the past few years I've been saying: it matters less what you do. It's about who you are when you're doing it. Sometimes you don't need to change your workplace, your city, or your relationship. You can learn to be your authentic self at home, at a party, at work — everywhere.

In the end it's about people, and who you choose to be in the world. The challenges don't end. But you know yourself and your values and your core so well — that the fear of losing yourself stops.

Home doesn't mean the journey is over.
It means the fear of losing yourself is.
