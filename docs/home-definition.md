# What "Home" Means — Yair's Words

*Written in response to feedback on the About section, February 2026.*

---

Home for me is the other side. in German it's called Jenseits. Coming back home for me means that most of the veil is lifted and we remember that everything is ok, we remember the bigger picture, we remember we are loved unconditionally by god, we remember we can have a daily connection with our immediate angels and guides, we remember that we are a soul within a soul family, we can live in two layers of consciousness at the same time - the one that experiences the specific physical and emotional life here, and the one that remembers that we designed the stage to learn something and come back home - make a full circle to inner peace, this time so much wiser and more appreciative of life and of our unique qualities - which we wouldn't be able to appreciate without going on that journey. For me, the challenges of life is like going away on a long trip. But if you never left your bed, let alone never left your doorstep - how would you ever get to experience the bliss of coming back home to take a shower and slip into bed with fresh sheets? How would you get to know yourself and your abilities if you never challenged anything? For me living with a consciousness of home is to be able to see someone, especially someone you love, even when you're mad at them, and see the soul that they are, see their fears and their passions and wishes and strengths, see the mirror they are to you and be able to be grateful for the growth they represent for you. Home is being able to feel layers of love that without being connected to your higher self, to the divine origine of your heart and to god - you coudn't even imagine existed. Home is seeing that we are in this together more than we could ever imagine before. Home is feeling calm at the eye of the storm, because your angels tell you that you are at the right place at the right time. Home is already having experience that when you listened to your inner voice, to your intuition, even if other people told you you were crazy, it payed off. Home is being able to gaze at a mountain and feel love and feel it loves you back. Home is feeling the love from the heart of mother earth. Home is being able to appreciate this moment. Like in the song "home" by Nicholas Bamberger: home is here, home is now.

And one more thing: home does not mean the journey is over. Home doesn't mean life's challenges are done and everything is la la land. In the past 2 years I've been saying: "It matters less what you do. It's about who you are when you're doing it." It means sometimes you don't need to change your work place to be who you are. You can learn to be your same authentic self at home, at a party, at work. In the end it's about people and who you choose to be in the world. It doesn't mean the challenges are over. It means that you know yourself and your values and your core vibration so well, that the fear to lose it stops - this is what ends. Not life, but the fear of losing yourself and regreting not taking the time to see the bigger picture while you were still alive.
